04 — Areas of Opportunity

Three things worth paying close attention to.

These aren't problems to panic about — they're opportunities to act on. Each one has a clear path forward, and each one becomes clearer with better measurement.

🇺🇸
The US deserves a fresh look
US revenue grew +0.2% while the market grew ~10%. Gymshark's largest territory is effectively standing still. The UK proves the brand works — so what's different in the US?

Without channel-level measurement by region, there's no way to tell whether the issue is too much spend going to saturated Meta audiences, not enough upper-funnel TikTok investment, or a post-restructuring gap after the Denver office closure. You can't fix what you can't see.

The NYC office relocation signals leadership is serious about US growth. The next step is regional ROAS data by channel — to rebuild the US media mix with evidence rather than intuition.
Priority: High — 41% of revenue at stake
⚡
Snapchat is wildly underinvested
This is the clearest data-backed opportunity. Snapchat delivered a +58% ROAS uplift — the highest of any measured channel — yet receives an estimated 5% of media budget.

Here's why this happens without unified measurement: Snapchat rarely gets the last click. Someone sees a Gymshark ad on Snapchat, doesn't tap it, then searches Gymshark three days later and buys through Google. Traditional tracking credits Google. Snapchat gets nothing. Budget goes to Google. The demand that drove the purchase gets defunded.

Fospha's MMM measures the impression — not just the click — and statistically isolates Snapchat's true contribution. That's how the +58% became visible. Without it, this opportunity stays buried.
Opportunity: Reallocation from saturated Meta → Snapchat
📊
£126M in spend, zero channel breakdown
Gymshark spent £126M on distribution in FY2024. That's verified in their Companies House filing. What isn't in any public document is how that £126M breaks down by channel.

Each platform's dashboard gives its own version of truth. Meta says it drove 800 conversions. TikTok says 600. Snapchat says 300. Total: 1,700. Actual sales: 900. Everyone is double-counting the same customers.

The result is budget decisions made on platform-reported ROAS — which systematically overcredits whoever gets the last click. With a 63% gross margin, Gymshark has the financial capacity to invest in solving this properly. The question is whether the £126M is being directed toward the channels that actually create demand.
Root cause: Attribution gap — solvable with MMM

05 — How Fospha Closes the Gap

The measurement science behind the insight.

Understanding how Fospha solves the attribution problem starts with the statistical model underneath it. This is genuinely elegant econometrics — applied to real marketing decisions.

The Regression Model
Revenue as the Dependent Variable
At its core, Fospha's Media Mix Model is a multivariate regression — the same framework you'd study in econometrics. Revenue is the dependent variable (what we're trying to explain), and each channel's spend level is an independent variable (a potential driver of that revenue). The model estimates how much each channel actually contributes to revenue — statistically, while controlling for everything else happening simultaneously.
Fospha MMM — Core Regression Formula
Revenue (Y) = β₀ + β₁(Meta Spend) + β₂(TikTok Spend) + β₃(Snapchat Spend) + β₄(YouTube Spend) + β₅(Influencer Spend) + β₆(Email Sends) + β₇(Seasonality) + β₈(Price Index) + β₉(Macro Conditions) + ε
Revenue Y — Dependent Variable
What we're trying to explain and predict. Every pound of revenue Gymshark earns is the output this model is trained on — week by week, across regions and channels.
β Coefficients
What the model solves for. Each coefficient tells you: for every £1 more spent on this channel, how much incremental revenue does Gymshark generate? That number is the true ROAS — statistically isolated, not platform self-reported.
Channel Spend — Independent Variables
Each channel's weekly spend level is an input the model uses to separate and attribute revenue contribution. When TikTok spend goes up and revenue goes up, the model quantifies exactly how much of that lift belongs to TikTok.
Control Variables
Seasonality (Black Friday), price changes, and macro conditions (inflation, consumer confidence) all affect revenue independently of marketing. Controlling for these isolates the true marketing effect from external noise.
ε — Error Term
The unexplained variation — the part of revenue the model can't attribute to any known variable. In a well-specified model this is small and randomly distributed, confirming the model isn't missing major drivers.
Why Not Simple OLS?
Standard OLS assumes linear relationships and no memory effects. Fospha uses Bayesian methods, adstock (lag effects), and saturation curves — making it far more accurate for the complex, overlapping reality of multi-channel marketing.
Beyond Basic Regression — 01
Adstock: Marketing Has Memory
A TikTok impression today might drive a purchase three weeks from now. Simple regression misses this entirely. Fospha models the carry-over effect of every channel so delayed conversions get attributed to the channel that created the demand — not just the one that happened to be last in the customer journey.
Beyond Basic Regression — 02
Saturation Curves: Diminishing Returns
Doubling Snapchat spend doesn't double revenue — at some point audiences saturate. Fospha uses Bayesian S-shaped saturation curves (not linear) to model exactly where each channel's returns start diminishing. This tells Gymshark precisely how far each channel's budget can go before efficiency drops off.
Beyond Basic Regression — 03
Daily Outputs: Act This Week, Not Next Quarter
Traditional MMM is run quarterly by consultants. By the time the report lands, the campaign is long over. Fospha runs the model daily at the ad level — so Gymshark's team can see on Tuesday that Snapchat ROAS jumped 40% and shift budget by Wednesday. That's the actionability gap, closed.
Proof in Practice — Gymshark x Fospha x Smartly (Nov 2025, verified)
When Gymshark integrated Fospha's full-funnel MMM data directly into Smartly's Predictive Budget Allocation tool, the results were measurable: +13% overall ROAS uplift across Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, and Pinterest. +39% on TikTok specifically. +58% on Snapchat. Gymshark's Head of Digital Marketing Daniel Green confirmed the integration "eliminated excessive time spent on budget decisions allowing us to focus more on creative strategy and growth." This is the regression formula above working in practice — coefficients estimated daily, budgets reallocated automatically, revenue improved measurably.

06 — Strategic Recommendations

Three moves that move the needle.

Every recommendation here is tied directly to verified data in this report. Specific actions, specific rationale, specific expected outcomes — not generic marketing advice.

01
Increase Snapchat budget allocation by 5-10pp
Snapchat is delivering +58% ROAS uplift on an estimated 5% of budget. The mismatch between performance and investment is the clearest budget reallocation opportunity in this analysis. The most likely source of reallocation is Meta retargeting — where audience saturation becomes a real risk at an estimated 35% budget share.
Expected outcome: If Snapchat maintains near-current ROAS efficiency at higher spend (validate with an incrementality test first), a 5pp reallocation from Meta could meaningfully improve overall blended ROAS across the paid mix — with no increase in total spend.
Tool: Fospha MMM → Beam (incremental forecasting) → Smartly PBA for automated reallocation
02
Run a US channel-level diagnostic with Fospha
US revenue growing +0.2% vs a market growing +10% cannot be diagnosed from public data. The question — paid media misallocation, brand awareness gap, or post-restructuring lag — requires regional channel ROAS data that only unified MMM can provide. Without it, any US strategy rebuild is educated guesswork at best.
Expected outcome: Regional Fospha data surfaces whether the US underperformance is concentrated in specific channels (Meta saturation? Insufficient TikTok upper funnel?) or reflects a systemic awareness gap — enabling a targeted, evidence-based rebuild rather than a broad budget increase.
Tool: Fospha Core → Regional channel ROAS breakdown → US-specific media mix rebuild
03
Protect TikTok and scale UGC creative systematically
TikTok is delivering +39% ROAS uplift — nearly 3x the cross-channel average. More importantly, it's the primary demand generation channel for Gymshark's core Gen Z demographic. UGC-style athlete creative drove 2.4x ROAS vs studio ads. Any budget review that cuts TikTok in pursuit of short-term Meta efficiency is trading long-term demand for short-term attribution comfort.
Expected outcome: Protecting TikTok while Fospha's impression-based MMM properly credits its upper-funnel contribution prevents the most common DTC measurement mistake: defunding the channel that builds demand because it never gets the last click — only to see Meta conversions mysteriously drop six weeks later.
Tool: Fospha Core → Impression modeling → TikTok Spark Ads + UGC creative scaling
